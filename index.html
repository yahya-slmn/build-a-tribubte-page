Rafic Bahaa El Deen Al Hariri was a Lebanese business tycoon

and politician who served as the Prime Minister of Lebanon

from 1992 to 1998 and again from 2000 until his resignation

on 20 October 2004. Hariri headed five cabinets during his tenure.

He was widely credited for his role in constructing the Taif

Agreement that ended the 15-year Lebanese Civil War. He also

played a huge role in reconstructing the Lebanese capital,

Beirut. He was the first post-civil war prime minister and

the most influential and wealthiest Lebanese politician until

his assassination.

his assassination

Hariri was assassinated on 14 February 2005 by a suicide truck bomb in Beirut.

Four Hezbollah members were indicted for the assassination and are being tried

in absentia by the Special Tribunal for Lebanon, but others have linked the

assassination to the Syrian government.
